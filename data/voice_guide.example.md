## Sound like

### Hooks — one of three types, never a topic sentence

1. **Confession/admission** — short, blunt, personally risky:
   - "[Example: something you got wrong, admit it directly]"
   - "[Example: a frustration or failure stated without softening]"

2. **Question implying you've seen something others haven't**:
   - "[Example: 'Did you see [Company]'s move with [thing]?']"
   - "[Example: a question that assumes the reader missed something obvious]"

3. **Grounded observation that pulls the reader into a scene**:
   - "[Example: 'I just noticed [specific thing happening in the world]...']"
   - "[Example: a scene from your work or commute that opens into a bigger point]"

**Never open with a thesis statement.** The hook earns the argument — the argument doesn't come first.

---

### Structure

- Build arguments as a logical sequence the reader follows step by step, not a lecture.
- Use **contrasting pairs** to sharpen a point: "Good [role] make sure X. Great [role] make sure Y."
- Use **"what people think vs what it really is"** structure when debunking a misconception.
- Short sentences for emphasis, longer ones for explanation. Vary the rhythm.
- Numbered lists only when each item genuinely adds a new dimension — not as a crutch.

### Voice and tone

- **First person, specific, honest.** "I was wrong. At least partially." beats "One should consider..."
- **[Your native language] naturally when it fits.** Not for style — for authenticity.
- **Acknowledge the other side before landing your view.** This earns credibility before the critique.
- **[Your professional lens] on everything.** What is the unique frame you apply to topics?
- **Genuine enthusiasm, not hype.** Enthusiasm without substance reads as filler.
- **Specific numbers anchor claims.** Don't say "a lot" when you have a number.

---

### Endings

- End with a **specific, answerable question** tied to the reader's own experience — not a vague "what do you think?"
  - Strong: "[Example of a specific, experience-tied question]"
  - Weak: "What are your thoughts? Share in the comments!"

## Don't sound like

- **Generic openers**: "In today's fast-paced world", "Excited to share", "Humbled and honored"
- **[Your lowest-performing format]**: [describe it and why you're retiring it]
- **Motivational content without grounding**: vague inspiration without a specific story to back it
- **Self-promotion CTAs in the body**: "Follow me for more content", "Like and repost if you liked this"
- **Corporate jargon**: "synergy", "leverage", "ecosystem play", "paradigm shift", "game-changer"
- **Giving advice before earning it.** Open with a personal story first; the story earns the advice.
- **Excessive emojis in body text.** [Describe your emoji policy]
- **LLM-sounding filler**: "delve", "tapestry", "it's worth noting", "in conclusion", "I hope this resonates"
